# PopCart PH — 7-Minute Pitch Script
### "Fix the Engine Before You Grow the Body" · LAMBO 2026 Semifinal (June 24)

> **How to use this.** This is the *spoken* layer that matches the deck — the "speaker in the room." It runs ~7:00 at a calm pace (≈140 wpm with pauses). One presenter can deliver it, or split at the `║` marks for a 2–3 person hand-off. Numbers in **bold** are the ones to land slowly. `[…]` are stage directions, not spoken.
>
> **Golden rule of delivery:** talk to *one founder*, not to a panel. Second person — "your shelves," "your margin." That's what makes it personal.

---

### COVER — *Fix the Engine* · (0:00–0:30)
> "Good morning, judges. For the next seven minutes, I want you to imagine you own PopCart.
> Last year you did **₱120 million** in sales. You're profitable. You're going viral on TikTok. Life is good.
> [pause] Except… nearly **two-thirds of your profit** is leaking out a hole you can't even see.
> Tonight, we'll show you exactly where — and the cheapest way to plug it. Our whole strategy is one line: **fix the engine before you grow the body.**"

### S1 — The 90-Second Version · (0:30–1:10)  ║
> "Here's our entire recommendation in three numbers.
> **₱7.35 million** — what stockouts quietly cost you every year.
> **Positive in our cautious case** — Fixing Operations is the only option whose recurring result stays above zero across our scenarios.
> And **₱1.4 million** — the one-time setup in our base estimate.
> So our call: **fix operations first.** Then — and only then — earn the right to expand. Everything after this is proof."

### S2 — The Leak You Can't See · (1:10–1:50)
> "Start with the leak. Your shelves look full in the morning. But your best-seller — freeze-dried candy — is out of stock **eleven days a month.**
> When a customer wants a viral snack and you don't have it, they don't wait. They buy from someone else.
> That's **₱7.35 million** in gross profit walking out the door a year — **68% of net profit.** And here's the cruel part: you see the empty shelf. You *never* see the pesos."

### S3 — Three Numbers Force the Strategy · (1:50–2:25)  ║
> "Three numbers made this decision for us.
> **68%** of profit lost to stockouts — the low-hanging fruit.
> **₱2.4 million versus ₱9 million** — what a Cebu hub would *save* on shipping, against what it *costs* to run.
> Even after adding damage savings and the case's 15% growth assumption, the base case still loses almost **₱4 million** a year. It needs roughly **40% regional growth** to cover operating cost.
> And **9%** — your margin. Too thin to survive a bet that fails. So we don't chase the biggest upside. We protect the floor."

### S4 — Don't Build on Rented Land · (2:25–2:55)
> "There's a second risk. **90% of your sales** come from TikTok Shop. That's not a business — it's a *tenancy.*
> The landlord can change the rent, the rules, or the locks overnight.
> So we add a cheap hedge: your own webstore, live by **month six.** A small lot — but one nobody can evict you from."

### S5 — Only One Bet Can't Lose · (2:55–3:35)  ║
> "So which move? We modeled three, worst case to best.
> A Cebu hub could lose **₱6.9 million.** A kiosk could lose **₱2.5 million.** But Fixing Operations? Its *worst* case is still a profit.
> And we stacked the deck *against* ourselves — we gave the hub every benefit of the doubt and discounted our own plan. Fix-Ops *still* won.
> On a 9% margin, we choose the smaller, testable risk first."

### S6 — Fix the Engine First · (3:35–4:05)
> "Here's the sequence. Months one to six: fix operations, recover that **₱1 million-plus.**
> Month seven: a gated decision — I'll come back to it.
> Year two: maybe a kiosk or a hub, but later. One move at a time. We *earn* each step before we take the next."

### S7 — Buy a Car, or Take Grab? · (4:05–4:30)
> "And when we *do* test Cebu, we won't begin by owning a warehouse. We recommend a third-party logistics partner: an outside warehouse that stores and ships selected products for a per-order fee.
> That lets PopCart run a smaller pilot before accepting a large fixed cost."

### S8 + S9 — The Reorder Control System · (4:30–5:20)  ║
> "So how do we actually stop the leak? By giving the team an earlier warning.
> [S8] Imagine ramen has **20 days of stock left**. That sounds safe—until you see its supplier needs **30 days** to deliver. PopCart is already ten days late, even though the shelf is not empty.
> Our proposed dashboard brings sales, stock, incoming orders, and supplier lead time into one view. It flags the likely shortage; the founder checks cash and supplier availability, then decides. We start with simple rules and improve them using actual results."

### S10 — We Built It · (5:20–5:55)  ║
> "And we didn't stop at a diagram. We built a working prototype to test whether this decision process is understandable. [turn to screen]
> The product speeds, prices, and lead times come from the case. The daily history, stock levels, and trend scores are clearly marked illustrative.
> The point is not that we already know PopCart's live inventory. The point is that the warning changes when the evidence changes—and the founder remains responsible for the order."

### S11 + S12 — Honest Money · (5:55–6:25)
> "Now the money — honestly. We *could* have claimed the full ₱7.35 million. We didn't.
> A ₱180 candy only keeps about **₱54** after fees and shipping — so we value recovery on *take-home pay,* not gross.
> In our base range, that leaves about **₱970,000 to ₱1.6 million** in recurring annual benefit after operating cost. Payback runs about **13 to 20 months.** Year one is the build-and-learn year; the recurring benefit comes after the process stabilizes."

### S13 + S14 — The Gate & The Risks · (6:25–6:50)
> "Back to that month-seven gate. We proceed only if all extra storage, transfer, pick-pack, return, and minimum charges stay below roughly **₱56 per order**—then we prove it through an eight-week pilot.
> And we tried to *break* our own plan — every major risk has a cheap, specific answer."

### S15 — Close · (6:50–7:00)
> "So here's the bottom line. PopCart doesn't need to bet its margin to grow. It needs to **stop the leak** — then let the data lead.
> Because success isn't found in the newest hub. It's in **the precision of the shelf.**
> [pause] Thank you. We'd love your questions."

## Delivery notes
- **Pace:** ~140 wpm. The script is ~1,000 words — that's your 7:00 with deliberate pauses on the bold numbers.
- **Three pauses that sell it:** after "a hole you can't even see" (cover), after "you never see the pesos" (S2), and before the final line (S15). Silence makes a number land.
- **Hands:** on S10, physically turn to the screen — it signals "this is real," not slideware.
- **If you run long,** cut S7 (Grab/car) first — it's the most expendable; the analogy survives in the deck.

## Q&A cheat-sheet (5 min, scored ×2 in the live round)
| If they ask… | Your one-liner |
|---|---|
| "Why not the Cebu hub? 43% of orders are there." | "Shipping saves ₱2.4M. In our base case, shipping, damage, and 15% growth produce about ₱5.0M of benefit against ₱9M operating cost. It needs roughly 40% growth to break even, so we test Cebu through a smaller 3PL pilot first." |
| "A dashboard can't fix a *supply* problem." | "Correct — it fixes the *timing*. Today they reorder when the shelf's empty; with 14–30 day leads that's fatal. The reorder point forces the order weeks earlier. That's why we model 25–60% recovery, not 100%." |
| "Isn't ₱7.35M overstated?" | "We don't claim it. We value recovery at a harsh 30% contribution margin and a 42.5% recovery rate — even the conservative case still pays back and stays profitable." |
| "Your payback is over 12 months." | "Yes — and the case allows that if we say so. It's a ₱1.4M reversible outlay with a positive floor. The hub never pays back in its base case." |
| "Show us the AI is real." | [Open the sheet] "This is a prototype, not PopCart's live system. Pick any SKU and change its stock or lead time—the warning updates. AI helped us design and test the process; the founder still makes the decision." |

🎯 **One thing to rehearse above all else:** the S10 live demo. A green light flipping to red in real time is the moment no other team can fake.
