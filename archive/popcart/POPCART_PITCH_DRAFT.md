# ▍SECTION A — STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

## SLIDE 1 — The Bottom Line  `answers Q1–Q5 in brief`
**Headline:** Fix the engine first. Earn the right to expand.
**Standfirst:** *If you read one slide, read this one — the whole case in five answers.*

**On-slide body (a 5-row "decision card"):**

**Sequence:** ① Fix operations (Mo 1–6) → ② Cebu 3PL, *gated on a quote* (Mo 7+) → ③ defer kiosk & owned hubs to Year 2

# ▍SECTION B — WHY IT MAKES SENSE *(reading the exhibits)*

## SLIDE 2 — Five problems, one question  `Q1`
**Headline:** The founders named five worries. Only one can be fixed first.
**Standfirst:** *Before choosing a fix, we asked a sharper question: which problem is quietly the most expensive?*

**On-slide body (5 problems, each tagged to its source):**
1. Shipping to Visayas/Mindanao is costly — ₱155 / ₱185 per order *(Exhibit B)*
2. Delivery is slow — 5–7 days (CV), 7–10 days (Davao) *(Exhibit B)*
3. Products arrive damaged — 4.2% / 5.0% return rate *(Exhibit B)*
4. Stockouts on viral items *(Exhibit C)*
5. 90% of sales on one channel — TikTok Shop *(Exhibit D)*

**The turn:** *Shipping is the loudest cost. Stockouts are the quietest quantified opportunity — and the cheapest first move to test.*
> **Think-alongside:** we frame the whole "why" section as a hunt for the real culprit.

## SLIDE 3 — Follow the money  `data point 1`
**Headline:** Half of every peso is product cost. A fifth is shipping. Hold that thought.
**Standfirst:** *Read Exhibit A with us — where does PopCart's ₱120M actually go?*

**On-slide body (Exhibit A waterfall / table):**
- Revenue **₱120.0M** → COGS **(₱60.0M, 50%)** → Gross profit **₱60.0M**
- − Platform fees & marketing **(₱12.0M, 10%)**
- − **Logistics & shipping subsidies (₱24.0M, 20%)** ← the single biggest controllable line
- − Warehouse OpEx (₱10.0M) − Admin (₱3.2M) → **Net profit ₱10.8M (9%)**

**The obvious instinct:** *Shipping is 20% of revenue. So cut shipping — build a regional hub. It feels right. Watch what happens when we test it.*
**Source:** Exhibit A (FY2025 Financial Snapshot)
> **Setup for the trap:** we let the reader reach the "build a hub" conclusion — then dismantle it two slides later. That's the ponder moment.

## SLIDE 4 — The leak you can't see  `data point 2 · Q1 answer`
**Headline:** Stockouts quietly cost ₱7.35M a year — 68% of net profit.
**Standfirst:** *Now read Exhibit C. Do this one calculation with us.*

**On-slide body (Exhibit C, worked live):**
- Freeze-dried candy: **300 units/day**, out of stock **11 days/month** *(Exhibit C)*
- 300 × 11 × 12 = **39,600 units** never sold → × ₱180 = ₱7.13M revenue → × 50% margin *(Exhibit A)* = **₱3.56M** lost on *one product*
- Across all fast-movers: **₱7.35M/yr in lost gross profit** = **68% of net profit** *(Exhibit A)*
- Exhibit D shows why the problem is difficult to control: inventory is updated by hand twice a day, while founders reorder through visual checks and gut feel.

**The realization:** *Shipping is the biggest cost. Stockouts are the biggest quantified recoverable opportunity in the case — and the lower-risk first move.*
**Visual:** leaking-bucket illustration · bar chart of lost profit by SKU (Exhibit C)
**Source:** Exhibit C (Inventory & Product Velocity), Exhibit A (margin), Exhibit D (manual ops)
> **The aha.** This is the emotional + analytical center of the deck.

## SLIDE 5 — Why the obvious move backfires  `data point 3 · Q2 reasoning`
**Headline:** A hub saves ₱2.4M in shipping — and costs ₱9M to run.
**Standfirst:** *Remember the "build a hub" instinct? Let's put real numbers on it.*

**On-slide body (Exhibit B + Assumptions):**
- Central Visayas: 48,100 orders/yr *(Assumption B)* at ₱155/order *(Exhibit B)*
- A hub cuts shipping ~₱50/order *(Assumption D)* → 48,100 × ₱50 = **₱2.4M saved/yr**
- Base-case damage savings add only ~**₱0.29M**; 15% growth adds ~**₱2.34M** in gross profit.
- Total base benefit is ~**₱5.04M**, versus **₱9M** annual operating cost *(Assumption C)*.
- **The question the data forces:** *why accept ₱9M of annual fixed cost when the base benefits total only ~₱5.04M?*
- Under base economics, the hub needs roughly **40% regional growth** to cover annual operating cost — above the case's **15–30%** planning range *(Assumption E)*.

**The constraint that settles it:** PopCart's margin is **9%** *(Exhibit A)* — too thin to survive a fix that costs more than it saves.
**Source:** Exhibit B, Assumptions B/C/D/E, Exhibit A
> **Payoff of the S3 setup.** The reader proposed the hub; the data talks them out of it. They now *trust* our reasoning.

## SLIDE 6 — The options, side by side  `≥3 options · Q2`
**Headline:** Only one option keeps a positive recurring floor in our model.
**Standfirst:** *We modeled each option's worst case — because on a 9% margin, the downside is what kills you.*

**On-slide body (range chart — recurring net profit/yr, worst → best):**
- **Fix Operations:** +₱0.2M → +₱3.5M — recurring floor stays positive
- **Cebu hub (owned):** −₱6.9M → +₱1.0M — loses in 2 of 3
- **Mall kiosk:** −₱2.5M → +₱3.0M — a coin-flip on foot traffic *(Assumption F)*
- **Decision rule:** protect the downside first; earn the upside through measured pilots.
- *(We even credited the hub's growth generously at 50% margin and discounted our own plan to 30% — Fix-Ops still wins.)*

**Source:** model built on Exhibit C, Assumptions C/D/E/F · full math in Appendix
> **Think-alongside:** the chart lets the judge *see* the asymmetry and agree before we say it.

# ▍SECTION C — HOW IT WILL WORK *(operations)*

## SLIDE 7 — The strategy: fix the engine first  `Q2 answer`
**Headline:** One primary move, one cheap hedge, one gated pilot — never all at once.
**Standfirst:** *We earn each step before we take the next.*

**On-slide body (3 phase cards):**
- **PRIMARY · Mo 1–6 — Fix operations:** build reliable stock visibility and earlier reorder warnings. Expected recurring benefit: ~₱1.0–1.6M/yr.
- **SUPPORTING · Mo 4–6 — Test a webstore:** launch a small second channel and measure real first-party demand; do not claim diversification before sales move.
- **GATED · Mo 7+ — Cebu 3PL pilot:** only if a real quote and an 8-week pilot prove savings after all partner costs.
- **YEAR 2 · deferred — Kiosk / owned hub:** evaluated later, not funded in Year 1.
- **The principle:** earn the right to expand — Year 1 builds operating proof and data that make Year-2 growth safer.
> **Resolves the "do everything" trap the case explicitly penalizes.**

## SLIDE 8 — The system that stops the leak  `Q3`
**Headline:** Give the team an earlier warning — while there is still time to act.
**Standfirst:** *Today PopCart reorders on gut feel (Exhibit D). With 14–30 day lead times (Exhibit C), that's already too late.*

**On-slide body (workflow, 5 steps):**
1. **UPDATE** — bring daily sales, current stock, incoming orders, and supplier lead times into one sheet.
2. **COMPARE** — ask a plain question: *will current and incoming stock last until the next delivery arrives?*
3. **WARN** — flag products likely to run short and show the projected gap.
4. **DECIDE** — the founder checks cash, supplier availability, and minimum order size before approving.
5. **LEARN** — compare alerts with actual results each week and improve the rules gradually.

**Starting tools:** Excel + Power Query and a barcode tool, building on the team's current Excel process. No custom app is required.
**Later use of AI:** once the data is reliable, add trend signals to help identify unusual demand—not to make purchasing decisions automatically.
**Source:** Exhibit D (current process), Exhibit C (lead times)
> **Highest-weighted criterion (×5).** Concrete workflow + real tools beats buzzwords.

## SLIDE 9 — We didn't just design it. We built it.  `Q3 proof`
**Headline:** We tested the proposed decision process with a working prototype.
**Standfirst:** *Case baselines go in; illustrative operating data demonstrates how the warning would work.*

**On-slide body:** screenshot of the live dashboard —
- **Example decision:** ramen has about **20 days of stock cover**, but its supplier needs **30 days** *(Exhibit C)*. The warning appears before the shelf is empty.
- Case-based inputs: product velocity, price, and supplier lead time. Illustrative inputs: daily history, stock on hand, and trend score.
- The output is a recommendation for review—not an automatic purchase order and not a claim about PopCart's actual live inventory.

**Source:** prototype built on Exhibit C data · README documents every formula (Appendix B)
> **Credibility spike.** This is the slide no other team can fake.

## SLIDE 11 — Where to expand — and when  `Q2 detail`
**Headline:** If PopCart enters Cebu, test the route before owning the warehouse.
**Standfirst:** *A third-party logistics partner stores and ships selected products for a per-order fee.*

**On-slide body:**
- An owned hub carries ₱7.2–10.8M in annual operating cost whether volume arrives or not *(Assumption C)*.
- A 3PL converts much of that commitment into per-order charges and allows a smaller pilot.
- **Cebu before Davao:** higher volume (26% vs 17%, *Exhibit B / Assumption B*), shorter distance.
- **The gate:** incremental storage, transfer, pick-pack, return, and minimum charges must remain below roughly **₱56/order** in the base case.
- **The proof:** run an 8-week pilot on selected fast movers; measure total cost/order, delivery time, damage, and stock availability.
- **Channel hedge:** own webstore by Mo 6 — *Exhibit D* shows almost no presence off TikTok today.

**Visual:** Grab-vs-car illustration · rented-land motif for channel
> **Q2 fully answered:** where (Cebu, via 3PL), and *when* (only when the unit economics earn it).

# ▍SECTION D — FINANCIAL LOGIC  `Q5`

## SLIDE 12 — The honest money
**Headline:** We value the win on take-home pay, not gross.
**Standfirst:** *We could have claimed the full ₱7.35M. Here's the number we'd actually keep.*

**On-slide body:**
- **Gross opportunity:** ₱14.70M lost sales × 42.5% recovery = ~**₱6.25M recovered revenue**.
- **Money left after product, platform, and shipping costs:** at 30–40%, ~**₱1.87–2.50M/yr**.
- **Less annual system cost:** **₱0.90M** → recurring benefit of ~**₱0.97–1.60M/yr**.
- **One-time setup:** **₱1.40M** *(within Assumption C's combined system/tool ranges)*.
- **Additional operational need:** confirm cash for earlier inventory purchases before approving each order.

**Visual:** lost-profit bars (Exhibit C) · contribution waterfall (₱180 → ₱54)
**Source:** Exhibit A, Exhibit C, Assumption D
> **Honesty is scored.** Discounting our own upside is what makes the number believable.

## SLIDE 13 — Payback without pretending
**Headline:** ~13–20 month expected payback; slower if fewer sales are recovered.
**Standfirst:** *The range matters more than a single impressive number.*

**On-slide body:**
- Payback by contribution margin: 30% → ~20 mo · 40% → ~13 mo · 50% → ~11 mo *(incl. 3-month build)*
- **Year 1 is an investment year:** approximately −₱0.67M under the base ramp assumption; recurring benefit follows after stabilization.
- The case explicitly allows a payback beyond 12 months if stated honestly — we state it.
- Compare: the hub never pays back in its base case; the kiosk's base payback is 115 months *(Assumption C/F)*.

**Visual:** payback sensitivity bars · plant→tree

# ▍SECTION E — ROADMAP, RISK & IMPACT

## SLIDE 14 — The 12-month roadmap
**Headline:** Build the habit first, then test the expansion.
**Standfirst:** *Each phase produces evidence for the next decision.*

**On-slide body (timeline):**
- **M1 · Establish the truth:** count inventory; clean the SKU and supplier list; confirm actual lead times; record baseline stockout days.
- **M2–3 · Pilot the warning:** use the five case fast movers; owner = warehouse manager + two admins; output = daily action list and logged founder decisions.
- **M4–6 · Stabilize and learn:** expand to fast movers; reach consistent two-hour updates; target stockouts toward 6–8 days; launch a small webstore test and measure actual orders.
- **M7 · DECISION GATE:** collect real 3PL quotes. Proceed only if all-in incremental cost is below the expected ~₱56/order benefit and PopCart can fund split inventory.
- **M8–9 · If GO, run an 8-week Cebu pilot:** selected SKUs only; track total cost/order, delivery time, damage, and stock availability. If NO-GO, remain centralized.
- **M10–12 · Decide and document:** compare pilot results with Exhibit B targets; write SOPs; approve or reject Year-2 expansion and kiosk testing.
> **The roadmap does not promise success at every phase. It creates evidence and gives the founders clear stop points.**

## SLIDE 15 — Risks, impact & the close
**Headline:** Stop the leak. Earn the growth.
**Standfirst:** *We tried to break our own plan — here's where it held.*

**On-slide body:**
- **Risks → mitigations (red-team):** staff adoption → low-code + training · channel shock → webstore by Mo 6 · recovery below model → already at harsh 30%, still positive · hub under-use → deferred behind a gate · long leads (ramen 30d, *Exhibit C*) → pre-order + 2nd supplier.
- **Expected impact:** stockouts 11 → ~6 base *(range ~4–8)* · updates 2×/day → every 2h · recurring annual benefit ~₱0.97–1.60M · net margin moves from 9% toward roughly **9.3–9.8%** before any successful 3PL upside.
- **Close:** *"Success isn't found in the newest hub — it's in the precision of the shelf."*
> **The payoff line ties back to the cover** — the engine, fixed.
